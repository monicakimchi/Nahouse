“people might think we’re hippies” Soma once joked. But if this lifestyle makes them "hippies", they’re hippies with rules — soft, undefined ones that I’m still figuring out and have surely fumbled with as a guest.

I was confused at how Rintaro-san cooked pasta, only to say he’d already eaten and offer it to me and Peter (“I cooked for him yesterday,” Peter explained). If I hadn’t taken that plate, would he have eaten that (very delicious) meal?

And I’m still learning a system wherein various friends and guests come and go, paying for their stay with household gifts or beer. Have the sake and snacks I’ve bought been enough too?
Each flyer, poster, drawing, random thing stuck to the wall must have some kind of memory attached to it. JJ laughs as he points out one letter he wrote to someone here; it’s now stuck to the bathroom door.

The walls scream nostalgia, and it’s charming yet alienating. The first time I walked into Nahouse and took in all these remnants of a vibrant past, I felt horribly foreign and lonely.

Less so now…I’ve stuck my own memory to the wall. Another friend, come and gone during the 2019 months of June and July.
I asked Miki how often they organize their parties, only to have her shrug and explain that they “just happen”.

They really do "just happen", and often too. Someone will start fiddling with the dj table, cans of beer will come out, the projector screen lowered, table pushed to the side, a dance floor formed.

Despite these festivities, the house is always clean and the kitchen spotless. The morning after a party, I’ll find trash bags outside the door – one full of beer cans, another of PET bottles, another of combustibles. Perhaps the wall will also have one extra flyer decorating it — like the Wakage no Itarimura brochure Gushiken-san showed Miki last night.
