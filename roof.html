“My favorite memory is the pool”

Miki shows me a photo and I laugh. I’m charmed yet confused by this house that resists separation and privatization,
that by its very layout brings people together to dance, smoke, cook,
and spontaneously blow up inflatable pools on the roof.

I’ve only interacted with a tiny portion of Nahouse’s 7 year life span —
Right at its end, just a month before its members have to move out
and find other homes in Naha.

“sabishii desu ne” I say.
How lonely. I meant to say “sad”, but mixed up the words.

“sabishii,” Miki agrees.

She tells me that the landlord plans to turn it
into something more profitable, probably a hotel. Ironic.
